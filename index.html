Egyptienne URW

Egyptienne is a slab serif typeface designed by Adrian Frutiger in 1956. The design was influenced by the Clarendon model of slabs that originated in 1845. Egyptienne has a large x-height and unlike Clarendon, contains italics so it can work well for setting body text. The typeface has been a classic go-to slab serif for over 50 years but doesn't seem to be used as much on the web, with other humanist slabs such as Adelle, being a more popular one on the web. Eventually, after some time, URW Egyptienne was created by the URW Type Foundry based in Hamburg, Germany. The URW foundry itself has more than 500 font families, and was later on bought and now owned by Monotype since 2020.
